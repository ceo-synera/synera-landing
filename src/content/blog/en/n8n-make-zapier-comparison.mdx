## Why Choosing the Right Tool Matters

Automation platforms are not interchangeable. Choosing the wrong one means overpaying, hitting technical limits when you need them most, or spending months learning something that doesn't fit your business.

Zapier, Make, and n8n are the three most widely used platforms in the world for automating workflows. All three connect applications and automate tasks, but they have very different philosophies, pricing, and capabilities.

## Zapier: The Easiest, The Most Expensive

Zapier pioneered making automation accessible to non-technical users. Its interface is simple, it has the most integrations (over 6,000 apps), and it's configurable in minutes.

**Who it's for**: companies that want simple automations without touching code, with budget to pay for convenience.

**The good**: ease of use, excellent support, proven reliability, the app you're looking for is probably already integrated.

**The bad**: pricing scales fast. The free plan allows 100 tasks per month, which runs out quickly. Paid plans start at USD 19/month and rise rapidly with volume. For complex or high-volume automations, Zapier can cost USD 500 or more per month.

**Key technical limitation**: Zapier is designed for linear flows (if X happens, do Y). Flows with complex branches, loops, or advanced conditional logic become difficult to maintain.

## Make (formerly Integromat): The Balance

Make offers a visual canvas-style interface where you see flows as a diagram. It's more complex than Zapier but much more powerful for non-linear flows.

**Who it's for**: technical or semi-technical teams that need complex automations without full programming.

**The good**: powerful visual interface, excellent handling of complex data structures (arrays, nested objects), much more reasonable pricing than Zapier for medium-high volumes, good webhook support.

**The bad**: steeper learning curve than Zapier, some integrations are less robust, the interface can get confusing in very large flows.

**Pricing**: free plan with 1,000 operations/month. Paid plans from USD 9/month. For most SMBs, Make is 3 to 10 times more economical than Zapier for the same automation volume.

## n8n: The Most Powerful, The Most Technical

n8n is open source and can be self-hosted on your own server. That changes everything: no operation limits, no costs that scale with volume, and full control over your data.

**Who it's for**: teams with technical capacity or companies that process large data volumes and don't want platform costs scaling with usage.

**The good**: open source and self-hostable (fixed cost, no volume limits), allows writing JavaScript code in any node, best capabilities for complex flows and data processing, excellent for non-standard API integrations.

**The bad**: requires technical knowledge to install and maintain the server, fewer native integrations than Zapier or Make (though it has hundreds), the cloud version has similar pricing to Make.

**Cloud pricing**: from USD 20/month. Self-hosted: server cost (from USD 5/month on a basic VPS) plus technical time for maintenance.

## What We Use at Synera and Why

At Synera we primarily work with n8n and Make, depending on the case:

**n8n** for projects requiring complex logic, large volume processing, custom API integrations, or where the client wants full control over their data. 70% of our projects run on n8n.

**Make** when the client has a team that will maintain the automations internally and we prefer a gentler learning curve.

**Zapier** rarely — only when the client already uses it and migration doesn't make sense, or when we need an integration that only exists in Zapier.

## The Right Question Isn't "Which Is Better"

It's: for what use case, with what team, and what budget?

If you're a non-technical person who wants to connect Gmail with Slack: Zapier.

If you have a technical team and want to automate complex business processes with full control: n8n.

If you're in the middle: Make is probably your best option.
