## Why Telegram Is an Underrated Platform for Business

Most companies think of WhatsApp when talking about business messaging. But Telegram has technical and cost advantages that make it especially attractive for certain business use cases.

Telegram's API is completely free with no volume restrictions. WhatsApp charges per business-initiated conversation. For companies with high message volume or active customer communities, this can represent significant savings.

Additionally, Telegram bots have native capabilities that in WhatsApp require additional solutions: interactive buttons, command menus, groups where the bot can actively participate, channels for mass communications.

## Most Effective Use Cases

**Internal technical support**: many companies use Telegram bots as assistants for their own teams. The bot answers team FAQs, provides access to internal information, notifies about important events. More efficient than a Slack channel or internal emails for certain communications.

**Notifications and alerts**: when something important happens in your business (a big sale, a system alert, a new lead), the bot notifies you instantly on Telegram. More immediate than email, more organized than a group chat.

**Customer communities**: Telegram groups with bot moderation. The bot can answer FAQs, moderate content, welcome new members with useful information, and send regular content.

**Sales channel**: for products or services where customers are already on Telegram, the bot can manage the complete sales cycle — initial inquiry, product information, order confirmation, post-sale follow-up.

**Report automation**: the bot sends daily or weekly reports to internal channels or groups. Sales metrics, project status, business KPIs — all in the place where the team already is.

## How It Works Technically

Telegram bots communicate through Telegram's official Bot API, which is REST and completely free. You can create a bot in minutes with BotFather, the official bot management bot.

Unlike WhatsApp Business API (which requires verification, business phone number, and Meta approval), any Telegram account can create a bot without an approval process.

At Synera we build the bot logic using n8n or custom code, connected to the Telegram API for receiving and sending messages, and to AI models for natural language processing.

## Telegram Bot vs WhatsApp Bot: Which to Choose

It's not a competition — it depends on the case:

**Choose Telegram if**:
- Your customers are already on Telegram or you're willing to bring them there
- You need high message volume without variable costs
- You want advanced functionality (groups, channels, complex buttons)
- It's for internal use (the team can adopt Telegram easily)

**Choose WhatsApp if**:
- Your customers are on WhatsApp and you don't want to ask them to switch apps
- It's for b2c customer service where app-switching friction is a problem
- Message volume is moderate and API cost isn't an issue

## Implementation: What You Need

To have a Telegram bot running you need:

1. **Telegram account and bot token** (free, 5 minutes with BotFather)
2. **Server or cloud service** to host the bot logic (from USD 5/month)
3. **Knowledge base** with the information the bot will use to respond
4. **Integration with your systems** if the bot needs to access your business data

In total, a basic Telegram bot can be running in 3 to 7 days at a considerably lower cost than alternatives on other platforms.
