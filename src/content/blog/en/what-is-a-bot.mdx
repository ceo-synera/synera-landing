## The simple definition

A bot is a program that executes tasks automatically, without a person having to do them manually every time.

An AI bot is a bot that can also understand natural language, learn from interactions, and make intelligent decisions based on context. It doesn't follow a rigid script — it understands what you say and responds coherently.

The difference matters. A basic bot responds "Our hours are 9 to 6." An AI bot responds "Today is Wednesday and it's 5:30 PM, so we're still open for another 30 minutes. Would you like me to confirm an appointment for tomorrow at 10?"

## How it works under the hood (no jargon)

The core of a modern AI bot is a large language model (LLM) — the same type of technology behind ChatGPT.

But there's a crucial difference: the bots we build at Synera aren't generic ChatGPT. They're systems trained specifically for your business, with your information, your communication style, and your processes.

When someone writes to the bot, the system understands the message's intent, searches the knowledge base for relevant information, formulates a response in the configured tone and style, and learns from the interaction to improve future responses.

## Basic bot vs. AI bot

A basic bot is like a call center options menu: frustrating, limited, and easy to break if the user phrases something unexpectedly.

An AI bot is like talking to an employee who knows your business. It handles ambiguity, understands typos, reads context, and gives answers that actually make sense for each user's specific situation.

The customer experience is completely different.

## Use cases by industry

**Accounting and finance**: Answering client queries about deadlines, required documents, and tax procedures. Our Accounting Bot is a concrete example.

**Healthcare**: Managing appointments, answering FAQ about services, sending medication and appointment reminders.

**Retail and e-commerce**: Order tracking, inventory queries, post-sale support, abandoned cart recovery.

**Professional services**: Initial lead qualification, answering basic inquiries, automatic meeting scheduling.

**Education**: Student support, resolving frequent doubts, providing access to materials and resources.

If you've never implemented a bot, the smartest first step is identifying which problem you want to solve.

Ask yourself: What repetitive tasks do I or my team do every day? What questions do clients always ask us? Where do we spend the most time on things that are always the same?

The answers to those questions are your starting point for designing a bot that has real impact — not just one that's technologically interesting but doesn't change anything about how the business actually runs.
