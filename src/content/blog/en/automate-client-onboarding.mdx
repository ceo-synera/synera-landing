## Why Onboarding Is the Most Important and Most Neglected Process

The first 30 days of a new client determine whether they stay or leave. Companies with a structured onboarding process have retention rates 2 to 3 times higher than those without.

And yet, onboarding remains one of the most manual, inconsistent, and chaotic processes in most companies. Each client is welcomed differently depending on who's available, how busy the team is, and whether someone remembered to send the right emails.

Automation solves exactly this: it ensures every client gets the same flawless welcome experience, on time, without anyone having to remember to do anything.

## What Stages a Well-Designed Onboarding Has

**Immediate confirmation**: the moment a client signs or pays, they receive automatic confirmation with next steps. Clarity from the very first second.

**Information request**: a smart form to collect what the team needs to get started. The bot can follow up if the client doesn't complete the form.

**Team introduction**: a personalized email or message introducing the person who will work with that client. This humanizes the relationship from the start.

**Access to tools and resources**: platform invitations, document access, tutorial links — all delivered in an ordered sequence, not all at once.

**Automatic 7-day check-in**: a message asking how things are going. Catches problems before they become cancellations.

**Onboarding period close**: at 30 days, a communication marking the end of the onboarding stage and the beginning of the normal relationship.

## How Each Stage Is Automated

**Initial trigger**: when a sale is recorded in your CRM, when a contract is signed in DocuSign/PandaDoc, or when a payment is processed in Stripe. This triggers the entire flow.

**Automatic communications**: emails and WhatsApp or Telegram messages sent at the right moments, with the right content based on client type and contracted service.

**Automatic internal tasks**: create the project in the management system, assign the responsible team, create the Slack channel or WhatsApp group, add the client to the CRM with correct data.

**Smart follow-up**: if the client doesn't complete the form within 48 hours, the bot sends a reminder. If they don't open the access email, the team receives an alert.

**Profile-based personalization**: the flow can branch based on client type, contracted plan, preferred language, or any variable that defines their ideal experience.

## Tools Used to Implement It

The most common combination at Synera:

- **n8n** to orchestrate the complete flow and handle conditional logic
- **WhatsApp Business API or Telegram** for messaging communications
- **Resend or SendGrid** for transactional emails
- **Notion, Airtable, or Google Sheets** as process database
- **Client's CRM** (HubSpot, Pipedrive, Salesforce) integrated as the source of truth

## Real Example: Digital Marketing Agency

An agency with 15 employees and 40 active clients had a completely manual onboarding. The process took 2 to 4 hours of administrative work per new client, with inconsistencies — some clients took 3 days to receive their access, others had it in 2 hours.

After automating the flow:
- Onboarding admin work time: from 3 hours to 20 minutes (review and approval)
- Consistency: 100% of clients receive the same experience
- Time to first value: reduced from 5 days to 2 days on average
- Satisfaction in the first 30 days: increased 40% according to NPS survey

The implementation ROI was recovered in the second month.

## What You Need to Get Started

Before automating, define:

1. What are the exact steps of your current onboarding?
2. What information do you need from the client to get started?
3. Which of those steps are always the same for all clients?
4. Where are the bottlenecks or most frequent errors?

With those answers, the technical implementation is straightforward. The hard part is process definition — and that's where we add the most value working with teams.
