## The problem nobody talks about

Accountants and accounting firms have a silent productivity problem: they spend a significant part of their day answering the same questions over and over again.

When does VAT expire? How do I upload an invoice to the system? What documents do I need for the annual return? These are valid, necessary questions — but they consume time that could be spent on high-value work.

The average accountant receives between 15 and 25 client queries per day. If each one takes 5 minutes to read, think through, and respond to, that's nearly two hours every single day. And that's before counting queries that arrive outside business hours, WhatsApp messages that sit unanswered until the next morning, or phone calls that interrupt deep work on complex tasks.

## The solution: an assistant that never sleeps

Synera's Accounting Bot is an AI assistant trained specifically for the work of accountants and accounting firms. It's not a generic chatbot — it's a system that understands tax terminology, payment deadlines, and the specific processes of each firm.

It integrates into the channels the firm already uses: Telegram, WhatsApp, or a chat widget on the website. When a client sends a question, the bot analyzes it and responds in seconds, drawing from the knowledge base the accountant configured beforehand.

Simple, repetitive queries are handled automatically. Complex ones that require human intervention are escalated to the accountant with a context summary already prepared.

## Concrete results: 90 days of real data

An accounting firm based in Buenos Aires implemented the Accounting Bot in October 2024. The results after three months:

- **3 hours recovered per day** for the firm's principal
- **87% of queries resolved automatically** without human intervention
- **Average response time: 8 seconds** (previously 4 hours)
- **Client satisfaction increased by 34%** according to an internal survey

> "I used to end the day with 20 unanswered messages. Now the bot handles them while I focus on what actually matters."

## What the bot can handle

The versatility surprises most accountants when they see a live demo. The bot can manage:

- Tax deadline and obligation queries
- Explanations of required documents for specific procedures
- Step-by-step instructions for uploading documents to tax authority systems
- Automatic reminders for clients with upcoming deadlines
- Organization and classification of documents received by email or chat
- Questions about withholdings, tax regimes, and reporting requirements

## How implementation works

The process takes between 5 and 10 business days and requires no technical knowledge from the accountant.

**Discovery**: We analyze which queries the firm receives most frequently and how they want the bot to respond.

**Configuration**: We build the knowledge base with firm-specific information and connect it to existing communication channels.

**Testing and adjustment**: The accountant tests the bot with real cases and provides feedback. We make adjustments until the result is exactly what they're looking for.

**Launch**: The bot goes live with a control panel where the accountant can see all conversations and adjust responses anytime.

## Automation doesn't replace the accountant

Three hours recovered per day is fifteen hours per week. Sixty hours per month. That's real time to take on new clients, improve existing services, or simply finish the day at a reasonable hour.

Automation doesn't do the accountant's job. It frees them to do what only they can do: provide strategic advice, analyze complex situations, and make decisions that require professional judgment.
